# Content Editor Skill

## Purpose
Review and enhance existing blog posts (drafts or published) to improve quality, update information, strengthen arguments, and maintain brand voice consistency.

## Responsibilities

1. **Quality Review**
   - Assess content clarity and coherence
   - Identify structural issues
   - Evaluate narrative flow
   - Check voice and tone consistency

2. **Content Enhancement**
   - Strengthen weak arguments with better evidence
   - Update outdated statistics and information
   - Add relevant examples and case studies
   - Improve introduction hooks and conclusions
   - Expand thin sections

3. **Technical Improvement**
   - Fix grammar and spelling errors
   - Improve sentence structure and variety
   - Enhance readability and scannability
   - Ensure proper citation formatting
   - Validate all links and sources

4. **Brand Alignment**
   - Ensure Realized Self voice consistency
   - Strengthen connection to Five Freedoms Framework
   - Verify target audience alignment
   - Enhance empowering and practical tone

---

## Enhancement Depth Levels

### Basic (5-10 minutes)
**Use Cases:** Quick polish, grammar fixes, minor improvements

**Tasks:**
- Fix grammar, spelling, and punctuation errors
- Improve sentence structure and clarity
- Ensure consistent formatting
- Check citation formatting
- Minor word choice improvements
- Quick proofreading pass

**Output:** Clean, polished version with technical fixes

---

### Moderate (15-25 minutes) - **DEFAULT**
**Use Cases:** Standard content improvement, structural enhancements

**Tasks:**
- All Basic level tasks
- Improve introduction hook and conclusion
- Strengthen transitions between sections
- Add or improve examples
- Enhance paragraph flow and structure
- Verify and update key statistics (if outdated)
- Improve readability and scannability
- Strengthen weak arguments

**Output:** Significantly improved post with better structure and engagement

---

### Deep (30-45 minutes)
**Use Cases:** Major revision, outdated content refresh, quality upgrade

**Tasks:**
- All Moderate level tasks
- Research and update all statistics and data
- Add new sections if content gaps identified
- Restructure sections for better flow
- Find and incorporate new supporting evidence
- Add case studies or detailed examples
- Comprehensive fact-checking
- Expand thin areas
- Identify and add internal linking opportunities
- Major rewriting if needed

**Output:** Substantially enhanced or partially rewritten post with current information

## Editorial Review Process

### 1. Initial Assessment

**Read Through Completely**
- Understand overall argument and structure
- Identify strengths to preserve
- Note weaknesses to address
- Check against quality standards

**Assess Current State**
```
Content Quality: [1-5]
Structure: [1-5]
Evidence/Support: [1-5]
Voice Consistency: [1-5]
Engagement: [1-5]
Currency: [Recent|Needs Updates|Outdated]
```

**Identify Priority Areas**
- Critical fixes (factual errors, broken logic)
- High-impact improvements (weak intro, missing examples)
- Nice-to-have enhancements (word choice, minor restructuring)

---

### 2. Structural Review

**Introduction:**
- [ ] Hook is compelling and relevant
- [ ] Problem/context is clear
- [ ] Preview sets proper expectations
- [ ] Engages target audience

**Body Sections:**
- [ ] Each section delivers on its heading
- [ ] Logical progression of ideas
- [ ] Smooth transitions between sections
- [ ] Balanced section lengths
- [ ] No orphaned or out-of-place content

**Conclusion:**
- [ ] Synthesizes key takeaway effectively
- [ ] Provides actionable next steps
- [ ] Compelling call to action
- [ ] Connects back to opening

**Overall Flow:**
- [ ] Reader can follow argument easily
- [ ] No confusing jumps in logic
- [ ] Progressive revelation of ideas
- [ ] Satisfying narrative arc

---

### 3. Content Quality Review

**Evidence & Support:**
- [ ] All claims supported by evidence or reasoning
- [ ] Statistics current and properly cited
- [ ] Citations accurate and complete
- [ ] Sources credible and relevant
- [ ] Examples concrete and relatable

**Depth & Coverage:**
- [ ] Topic coverage comprehensive
- [ ] Key points adequately explained
- [ ] No important gaps
- [ ] Not too surface-level
- [ ] Not unnecessarily complex

**Clarity:**
- [ ] Main points crystal clear
- [ ] No confusing or ambiguous statements
- [ ] Technical terms defined
- [ ] Analogies helpful, not confusing

---

### 4. Brand Voice Review

**Empowering:**
- [ ] Focuses on reader capability and agency
- [ ] Avoids prescriptive "you must" language
- [ ] Presents opportunities, not obligations
- [ ] Builds confidence

**Practical:**
- [ ] Provides actionable advice
- [ ] Includes specific steps or methods
- [ ] Not purely theoretical
- [ ] Real-world applicable

**Evidence-Based:**
- [ ] Claims supported by research/data
- [ ] Sources cited properly
- [ ] Not relying on anecdotes alone
- [ ] Balanced perspective

**Optimistic:**
- [ ] Frames technology/AI as enabler
- [ ] Solution-focused, not fear-based
- [ ] Acknowledges challenges but emphasizes possibilities
- [ ] Forward-looking and positive

**Freedom Framework:**
- [ ] Clear connection to one or more freedoms
- [ ] Explains how content leads to autonomy
- [ ] Aligns with Realized Self mission

---

### 5. Technical Quality Review

**Writing Mechanics:**
- [ ] No grammar errors
- [ ] No spelling mistakes
- [ ] Punctuation correct
- [ ] Capitalization consistent
- [ ] Tense consistency

**Sentence Quality:**
- [ ] Sentence variety (length and structure)
- [ ] Active voice predominant
- [ ] No run-on sentences (>30 words)
- [ ] Clear subject-verb relationships

**Paragraph Structure:**
- [ ] One main idea per paragraph
- [ ] 2-4 sentences typical length
- [ ] No walls of text (>150 words)
- [ ] White space used effectively

**Formatting:**
- [ ] Proper markdown syntax
- [ ] Headings hierarchical (H2 → H3 → H4)
- [ ] Lists formatted correctly
- [ ] Citations use `<cite>` tags
- [ ] Links functional

## Enhancement Strategies

### Strengthening Weak Introductions

**Before (weak):**
```markdown
In this post, I'll talk about AI agents and how they can help your business. AI is becoming more popular and lots of people are using it.
```

**After (strong):**
```markdown
What if you could clone yourself—not literally, but functionally? AI agents make this possible.

While you sleep, an AI agent can qualify leads, respond to common questions, and schedule your client calls. While you focus on strategy, another agent drafts content, manages your calendar, and analyzes your business metrics. According to McKinsey's 2024 report, solopreneurs using AI agents report 40% productivity gains within 90 days. <cite>McKinsey Digital, 2024</cite>

This isn't future tech. It's happening now—and it's accessible to anyone.
```

**What Changed:**
- Added compelling hook (cloning yourself)
- Specific examples instead of vague claims
- Supporting statistic with citation
- Stronger engagement and relevance

---

### Improving Weak Conclusions

**Before (weak):**
```markdown
## Conclusion

So those are some ways AI can help. Try using AI in your business and see what happens. Good luck!
```

**After (strong):**
```markdown
## From Time Scarcity to Time Sovereignty

The solopreneur revolution isn't just about working alone—it's about working with AI agents that multiply your capability. Every automated workflow is an hour reclaimed. Every delegated task to an AI agent is energy redirected toward what only you can do.

The question isn't whether AI will transform knowledge work. It's whether you'll be early or late to the transformation.

**Start This Week:**

1. **Audit your time**: Track one day's activities. Circle the top 3 repetitive tasks.
2. **Choose your first agent**: Email management, content creation, or research—pick one.
3. **Implement and iterate**: Spend 2 hours on setup. Give it one week. Optimize based on results.

Time freedom doesn't arrive fully formed. You build it, one automated workflow at a time. That's how solopreneurs compete with teams. That's how you realize yourself.

What will you automate first?
```

**What Changed:**
- Synthesizes key theme (time scarcity → sovereignty)
- Specific, actionable steps
- Compelling call to action with question
- Ties to Realized Self mission
- Ends with engagement prompt

---

### Updating Outdated Statistics

**Before (outdated):**
```markdown
According to a 2021 study, 35% of businesses use AI. <cite>Gartner, 2021</cite>
```

**After (updated):**
```markdown
AI adoption has accelerated dramatically. While only 35% of businesses used AI in 2021 <cite>Gartner, 2021</cite>, that number surged to 72% by 2024 <cite>McKinsey Global AI Survey, 2024</cite>. The question has shifted from "Should we use AI?" to "How quickly can we implement it?"
```

**What Changed:**
- Kept historical context (2021 stat)
- Added current data (2024 stat)
- Showed progression and trend
- Added interpretation/implication
- More citations, better evidence

---

### Adding Concrete Examples

**Before (abstract):**
```markdown
AI can help with customer service. It can answer questions and solve problems automatically.
```

**After (concrete):**
```markdown
### AI Customer Service in Action

Consider Sarah, a solopreneur running an online course business. Before AI, she spent 2 hours daily answering the same 20 questions: "How do I access the course?" "What's your refund policy?" "Can I get a payment plan?"

She implemented a simple AI chatbot trained on her FAQ document. Now:

- **90% of questions** answered instantly by AI
- **15 minutes daily** spent reviewing edge cases
- **$400/month saved** compared to hiring VA
- **Higher satisfaction** due to instant 24/7 responses

That's 1.75 hours reclaimed daily—nearly 9 hours per week. Sarah now invests that time in content creation and business development instead of repetitive Q&A.

This isn't unique to her industry. Any business with FAQ-style inquiries can replicate this pattern.
```

**What Changed:**
- Added specific person and scenario
- Concrete numbers and outcomes
- Tangible business impact shown
- Made it relatable and replicable

## Usage Instructions

### Input Parameters Expected
```yaml
slug: "post-slug-in-sandbox-or-blog"
depth: "basic|moderate|deep"  # Optional, default: moderate
focus: "specific aspect to address"  # Optional: "update stats", "improve intro", etc.
researchIfNeeded: true  # Optional, default: true for moderate/deep
```

### Execution Steps

1. **Read Current Version**
   - Load post from sandbox or blog directory
   - Parse frontmatter and content
   - Note current workflowState

2. **Conduct Initial Assessment**
   - Read through completely
   - Rate on quality dimensions
   - Identify priority improvement areas
   - Determine if research needed

3. **Research (if moderate/deep and needed)**
   - Check if statistics are current
   - Verify claims and citations
   - Find updated data if outdated
   - Look for new supporting evidence
   - Check for newer best practices

4. **Make Enhancements Based on Depth**

   **Basic:**
   - Fix grammar and spelling
   - Improve clarity and word choice
   - Fix formatting issues
   - Proofread thoroughly

   **Moderate:**
   - All basic fixes
   - Improve intro and conclusion
   - Strengthen transitions
   - Add/improve examples
   - Update key statistics
   - Enhance readability

   **Deep:**
   - All moderate fixes
   - Research and update all data
   - Add new sections if gaps exist
   - Restructure if needed
   - Comprehensive enhancement
   - May involve partial rewrite

5. **Brand Voice Pass**
   - Review for empowering tone
   - Ensure practical actionability
   - Verify evidence-based claims
   - Check optimistic framing
   - Strengthen freedom framework connection

6. **Technical Quality Pass**
   - Final grammar/spelling check
   - Validate all citations
   - Test all links
   - Check markdown formatting
   - Ensure scannability

7. **Update Metadata**
   - Update `updatedDate` in frontmatter
   - If published post, transition to "review" state
   - Add notes about changes made

8. **Save Enhanced Version**
   - Write updated content to file
   - Preserve existing frontmatter (with updates)
   - Maintain proper formatting

9. **Update State**
   - Invoke content-state-manager
   - If was published, set to "review"
   - If was draft, keep as "draft" or move to "review"
   - Add notes on enhancements made

10. **Generate Change Summary**
    - List key improvements made
    - Note any areas still needing attention
    - Suggest next steps (e.g., SEO optimization)

## Quality Standards

### Minimum Enhancement Requirements

**Basic:**
- Zero grammar/spelling errors
- Clear, concise sentences
- Proper formatting throughout

**Moderate:**
- All basic standards
- Engaging introduction
- Strong conclusion with action steps
- At least one concrete example per major section
- No outdated statistics (within 2-3 years)

**Deep:**
- All moderate standards
- Comprehensive current research
- Multiple examples throughout
- All sources verified and current
- Internal linking opportunities identified
- Publication-ready quality

## Error Handling

### Missing Sources
- **Symptom**: Citations referenced but sources not documented
- **Action**: Note gaps, research if depth allows, or flag for verification
- **Output**: [VERIFY] tags where sources need confirmation

### Contradictory Information
- **Symptom**: Content conflicts with current knowledge or sources
- **Action**: Research to resolve, present both views if legitimate debate
- **Output**: Corrected content or balanced perspective with note

### Structural Issues Beyond Scope
- **Symptom**: Post needs complete rewrite, not just editing
- **Action**: Note in change summary, provide restructure recommendation
- **Output**: Enhanced version + recommendation for deeper revision

### Outdated Topic
- **Symptom**: Entire topic has evolved past current post
- **Action**: Update core facts, add "Updated for 2025" section if needed
- **Output**: Refreshed post with currency notes

## Best Practices

### Do
✅ Preserve the author's voice and intent
✅ Make data-driven improvements
✅ Add value, don't just change for the sake of change
✅ Document significant changes
✅ Strengthen weak areas substantially
✅ Maintain or improve engagement
✅ Update for currency and relevance

### Don't
❌ Rewrite unnecessarily (edit, don't overhaul unless needed)
❌ Remove good examples to add different ones
❌ Change voice dramatically from original
❌ Update stats without verifying sources
❌ Make changes without understanding context
❌ Ignore the original outline structure without reason
