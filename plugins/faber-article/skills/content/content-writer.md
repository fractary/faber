# Content Writer Skill

## Purpose
Transform detailed outlines into complete, engaging blog posts that embody Realized Self's voice, maintain high quality, and provide actionable value to readers.

## Responsibilities

1. **Content Generation**
   - Write full blog post from outline
   - Maintain consistent voice and tone throughout
   - Ensure smooth narrative flow between sections
   - Meet target word count (800-2000 words)

2. **Brand Voice Implementation**
   - Empowering: Focus on individual capability and agency
   - Practical: Provide actionable insights and steps
   - Evidence-based: Incorporate research and data
   - Optimistic: Frame AI and technology as enablers

3. **Quality Assurance**
   - Engage readers with compelling hooks
   - Support claims with proper citations
   - Include relevant examples and case studies
   - Ensure proper markdown formatting
   - Proofread for clarity and coherence

4. **SEO and Structure**
   - Use headings hierarchically (H2, H3)
   - Write scannable content (bullets, short paragraphs)
   - Include keywords naturally
   - Craft compelling meta descriptions

---

## Realized Self Brand Voice Guide

### Tone Attributes

**Empowering**
- ✅ "You have the capability to..."
- ✅ "This puts you in control of..."
- ❌ "You need expert help to..."
- ❌ "This is too complex for most people..."

**Practical**
- ✅ "Here's exactly how to implement this..."
- ✅ "Start by taking these three steps..."
- ❌ "In theory, you could..."
- ❌ "Philosophically speaking..."

**Evidence-Based**
- ✅ "Research shows..." <cite>Source</cite>
- ✅ "According to a 2024 study..." <cite>Source</cite>
- ❌ "Many people say..."
- ❌ "It's common knowledge that..."

**Optimistic**
- ✅ "AI enables you to..."
- ✅ "This creates new opportunities for..."
- ❌ "AI threatens to..."
- ❌ "You're falling behind if you don't..."

### Writing Style Guidelines

**Sentence Structure**
- Mix short (10-15 words) and medium (20-25 words) sentences
- Avoid sentences over 30 words
- Use active voice predominantly
- Vary sentence beginnings

**Paragraph Length**
- 2-4 sentences per paragraph
- Break up long blocks of text
- Use white space effectively
- One main idea per paragraph

**Word Choice**
- Use "you" to directly address reader
- Choose concrete over abstract terms
- Prefer simple words over jargon (unless defining technical terms)
- Avoid buzzwords and clichés

**Engagement Techniques**
- Ask rhetorical questions to prompt reflection
- Use specific examples and scenarios
- Include relatable analogies
- Incorporate occasional subversive or contrarian takes

## Content Structure Template

### Introduction (150-250 words)

**Hook** (1-2 sentences)
- Compelling question that resonates with reader's pain
- Surprising statistic that challenges assumptions
- Relatable scenario that draws reader in
- Bold statement that intrigues

**Example:**
```markdown
What if I told you that 80% of your daily tasks could be automated by AI agents you build yourself—no coding required? <cite>McKinsey Digital, 2024</cite>

For solopreneurs and knowledge workers, time is the ultimate currency...
```

**Problem/Context** (2-3 paragraphs)
- Establish the challenge or question
- Show understanding of reader's situation
- Explain why this matters now
- Connect to broader theme of freedom/autonomy

**Preview** (1 paragraph)
- Brief overview of what's coming
- Tease the key insight or solution
- Set expectations for value reader will gain

---

### Main Content Sections (250-400 words each)

**Section Structure:**

**Section Heading** (H2: ##)
Brief introduction sentence connecting from previous section.

**Subsection 1** (H3: ###)
Content paragraph explaining the concept or point. Use specific examples and concrete details. <cite>Include citations</cite> where appropriate.

Supporting paragraph with additional context, evidence, or application. Keep paragraphs focused and scannable.

**Subsection 2** (H3: ###)
Continue developing the section theme with new but related point.

Include:
- Bullet lists for easy scanning when listing items
- Blockquotes for emphasis or notable quotes
- Examples that illustrate concepts concretely
- Data and statistics with citations

> "Quote that reinforces the point or provides expert perspective." <cite>Source Name</cite>

**Practical Application**
Tie the concepts back to reader's real-world scenario. Show them how to apply this.

---

### Conclusion (150-200 words)

**Key Takeaway** (1-2 paragraphs)
- Synthesize the main insight
- Connect back to opening hook (full circle)
- Empower reader with what they now know/can do

**Action Steps** (Bulleted list)
Concrete steps reader can take immediately:
1. **[Specific action]**: Brief description of what and why
2. **[Specific action]**: Brief description of what and why
3. **[Specific action]**: Brief description of what and why

**Call to Action** (1 paragraph)
- Invitation to go deeper (explore related content, try a tool)
- Question for reflection
- Connection to Realized Self's mission and freedoms

**Example:**
```markdown
Start with one automated workflow this week. Experience the shift from time scarcity to time sovereignty. That's how you begin building your freedom—one reclaimed hour at a time.

Which of your daily tasks will you automate first?
```

## Formatting Standards

### Markdown Syntax

**Headings:**
```markdown
## Main Section Heading (H2)
### Subsection Heading (H3)
#### Minor Heading if Needed (H4)
```

**Emphasis:**
```markdown
*Italic for gentle emphasis*
**Bold for strong emphasis or key terms**
```

**Lists:**
```markdown
**Unordered:**
- Item one
- Item two
  - Sub-item (indent with 2 spaces)

**Ordered:**
1. First step
2. Second step
3. Third step
```

**Blockquotes:**
```markdown
> This is a blockquote for notable statements or quotes.
> Can span multiple lines.

> "Direct quote with citation" <cite>Source Name, Year</cite>
```

**Links:**
```markdown
[Link text](https://url.com)
<cite>[Source Title](https://source-url.com)</cite>
```

**Code (if applicable):**
```markdown
`inline code`

```language
code block
```
```

## Citation Guidelines

### When to Cite
- All statistics and data points
- Research findings and study results
- Expert opinions and quotes
- Frameworks or methodologies from other sources
- Controversial or surprising claims

### Citation Format

**In-text:**
```markdown
According to a 2024 McKinsey study, AI agents could automate up to 70% of knowledge work tasks. <cite>McKinsey Digital, 2024</cite>
```

**With link:**
```markdown
Research from MIT shows that AI-augmented workers are 40% more productive. <cite>[MIT Sloan Review](https://example.com)</cite>
```

**Block quote:**
```markdown
> "The future belongs to those who combine human creativity with AI capability."
> <cite>Andrew Ng, AI Researcher</cite>
```

### Citation Accuracy
- ✅ Verify all citations match sources from research brief
- ✅ Include publication year when available
- ✅ Link to original source when possible
- ✅ Represent findings accurately (no distortion)
- ❌ Never invent citations or statistics

## Usage Instructions

### Input Parameters Expected
```yaml
outline: "Path to outline file in sandbox"
targetWordCount: 1200  # Optional, default from outline
tone: "standard"  # Optional: standard|authoritative|conversational
focus: "specific aspect to emphasize"  # Optional
```

### Execution Steps

1. **Read Outline**
   - Parse outline from sandbox file
   - Extract structure, main points, and assigned evidence
   - Note target word count and section estimates
   - Review research summary and sources

2. **Plan Approach**
   - Determine narrative style (problem-solution, framework, analytical, etc.)
   - Identify key examples and analogies to use
   - Plan tone modulation (serious vs. light moments)

3. **Write Introduction**
   - Craft compelling hook (choose best from outline options or create new)
   - Establish context and problem
   - Preview value and content
   - Aim for 150-250 words

4. **Write Main Sections**
   - Follow outline structure
   - Expand on each main point with depth and detail
   - Incorporate assigned sources and citations
   - Add examples and concrete applications
   - Maintain voice consistency
   - Use proper markdown formatting
   - Aim for target word count per section

5. **Write Conclusion**
   - Synthesize key takeaway
   - Provide 3 specific action steps
   - Include compelling call to action
   - Connect back to opening hook
   - Aim for 150-200 words

6. **Polish and Refine**
   - Read through for flow and coherence
   - Check all citations present
   - Verify markdown formatting
   - Proofread for errors
   - Ensure brand voice consistency
   - Confirm quality checklist items

7. **Update Frontmatter**
   - Keep existing frontmatter
   - Update workflowState to "draft"
   - Refine description if needed
   - Keep preliminary tags

8. **Save Draft**
   - Replace outline section with full content
   - Keep sources at bottom for reference
   - Ensure proper file formatting

9. **Update State**
   - Invoke content-state-manager
   - Transition from "outline" to "draft"
   - Add timestamp and notes

## Error Handling

### Outline Gaps
- **Symptom**: Missing details or incomplete outline sections
- **Action**: Fill gaps with logical content while noting areas that may need fact-checking
- **Output**: Complete draft with notes on what needs verification

### Insufficient Word Count
- **Symptom**: Following outline exactly results in 600-word post instead of target 1200
- **Action**: Expand with additional examples, deeper explanations, or relevant tangents
- **Output**: Meet target while maintaining quality and relevance

### Voice Inconsistency
- **Symptom**: Some sections sound off-brand or generic
- **Action**: Revise to match Realized Self voice guidelines
- **Output**: Consistent empowering, practical tone throughout

### Missing Citations
- **Symptom**: Outline assigned sources but they're not in research brief
- **Action**: Note gaps and write content assuming verification will come
- **Output**: Draft with [VERIFY] notes where citations needed

## Best Practices

### Do
✅ Write like you're talking to an ambitious friend
✅ Use concrete examples and real scenarios
✅ Break up text with bullets and short paragraphs
✅ Incorporate "you" to engage reader directly
✅ End sections with bridge sentences to next section
✅ Support claims with evidence
✅ Provide actionable takeaways

### Don't
❌ Use academic or overly formal language
❌ Create walls of text (paragraphs >150 words)
❌ Make claims without evidence
❌ Be prescriptive or condescending ("You must...")
❌ Use fear-based motivation
❌ Oversimplify complex topics
❌ Skip proofreading
